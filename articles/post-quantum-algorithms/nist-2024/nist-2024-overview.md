# The NIST 2024 Post-Quantum Standardization Project

## Overview

In 2024, NIST finalized its post-quantum cryptography standardization process, selecting four algorithms designed to resist attacks from quantum computers.

## The Four Standardized Algorithms

This section covers each of the four selected algorithms:

- **Kyber KEM** — A key encapsulation mechanism based on module lattices.
- **Dilithium** — A digital signature scheme based on module lattices.
- **Falcon** — A compact digital signature scheme based on NTRU lattices.
- **SPHINCS+** — A stateless hash-based digital signature scheme.

## Why It Matters

Classical public-key cryptography (RSA, ECC) is vulnerable to Shor's algorithm running on a sufficiently powerful quantum computer. The NIST 2024 standards provide drop-in replacements designed for long-term security.
